## Health Assessment of trees and green spaces based on Earth Observation

Understanding the health of trees and green spaces is essential for maintaining resilient ecosystems, supporting biodiversity, and ensuring the long term provision of ecosystem services in both urban and rural environments. Earth Observation enables largescale, repeatable, and objective assessment of vegetation conditions by analyzing indicators such as canopy vitality, moisture content, leaf area, and photosynthetic activity. By integrating these EO based insights, stakeholders can effectively track ecosystem dynamics, improve landscape management, and support evidence based environmental planning.



Following these principles, OHB‑DS provides a Health Index for individual trees and green spaces. This index is calculated as a weighted and normalized average of selected vegetation indicators, such as NDVI, NDMI, NDRE, and SVHI.
Based on the Health Index, a Health Change Index can be derived to assess the condition of individual trees or green spaces. This change index is a relative measure, as the assessment depends on a chosen reference value. Several options are available for defining this reference, including:
- the early‑summer optimum for a reference year, defined as the maximum value observed during May–June 2024, or
- the optimal monthly state for a reference year, based on evaluating the health index against corresponding monthly reference values.

Once a reference value is set, the Health Change Index is computed as the deviation of current index values from this defined reference.


## Products

The products below which OHB-DS can provide are based on Sentinel-2 indices at 10m spatial resolution which can be further improved by the super resolution methods to 2.5 m spatial resolutions:
- Health Analysis showing used time series data for different selected indices derived from satellite imagery
- Health Monitoring tracks the condition of vegetation through the Health Change Index.
- Yearly Health Comparison of the health index to allow trend analysis.
- Monthly Trend Index represents the rate of change based past years (e.g. five years) of aggregated health index data.
- The Overall Trend Index as the average of the monthly trend index considering the months April, May, June, July and August (can be updated).
- Geospatial data products at 10m spatial resolution (e.g. geoTiffs) as well as map layers for the health index, and visual indicating of the health assessment.  

As in intermediate layer access to these products can be provided by the existing interface https://vegetation.urbanview.ohb-ds.de/. Please contact ingo.schoolmann@ohb-ds.de in case access, adaptations or customization of the products is of interest.

## Other Advances

Another variant of a Health Index utilizes land surface temperature information, evapotranspiration and vegetation indicators, which leads to an additional assessment of health for trees and green spaces. During the ADUCAT project, this health index as well as the already developed health index solely based on Sentinel-2 can be considered.
